VER: Vision Expert Transformer for Robot Learning via Foundation Distillation and Dynamic Routing

We present Vision Expert Transformer for Robot Learning (VER), a novel framework that distills knowledge from multiple vision foundation models (DINOv2, ViT, CLIP) into a unified expert.

Abstract

While pretrained vision foundation models (VFMs) substantially advance robotic systems by providing rich visual representations, each individual VFM typically excels only within specific visual domains or functionalities, limiting their standalone effectiveness across diverse robotic tasks. Existing methods that distill multiple VFMs into a unified representation pose additional challenges, including the lack of flexibility in selecting task-specific representations and the necessity for full retraining when incorporating new VFMs or robot-specific domain knowledge. To address these challenges, we propose VER, a Vision Expert transformer for Robot learning. VER distills knowledge from multiple VFMs into a specialized expert library, and learns dynamic routing to selectively utilize task-relevant vision experts for downstream robot tasks. We further investigate the routing mechanism and observe that the router progressively learns to extract lower-entropy, structured task-relevant features, acting as an implicit planning module that alleviates the policy head\’s burden. Moreover, VER facilitates the seamless integration of new trainable experts, enabling the acquisition of robot-related domain knowledge beyond the pretraining dataset and continuously distilling new vision foundation models. Extensive experiments on 11 diverse robotic benchmarks demonstrate that VER achieves state-of-the-art performance with a 74.7% average success rate.

Method

Our framework consists of two main components: a Base Vision Transformer (BVT) that generates unified feature representations from input images, and a Vision Expert Library (VEL) comprising specialized experts that capture diverse visual representations from various vision foundation models.

Training consists of two phases that enables efficient knowledge distillation from diverse vision foundation models and adaptive feature selection for specific robotic tasks:

(1) Pretraining, where a Base Vision Transformer processes input images, and Teacher-Specific Routers in the Vision Expert Library select appropriate experts to mimic representations from foundation models;

(2) Downstream Robot Tasks, where we freeze the experts and train a lightweight Robot Router (< 0.4 parameters) that dynamically selects task-relevant visual features to guide the policy head in generating appropriate robotic actions. We also incorporate a noisy gating mechanism that promotes exploration during training.

Contributions:

  • We propose VER, which distills knowledge from diverse VFMs into a vision expert library and enables robots to autonomously select task-relevant experts to generate structured visual representations.
  • We analyze the routing process for downstream robotic tasks and observe that Robot Router is trained to generate lower-entropy, more structured representations, resulting in improved performance in downstream robotic tasks.
  • We Facilitates the seamless integration of new trainable experts, enabling the acquisition of robot-related knowledge beyond the existing dataset and continuously distilling new vision foundation models.

Experiments

Our approach outperforms previous state-of-the-art methods across 11 diverse tasks from Franka Kitchen, Meta-World, and Adroit environments, achieving the highest average success rate (74.7%).
