GeVER: General Vision Expert Transformer for Robot Learning via Foundation Distillation and Dynamic Routing

Anonymous Authors
In Submission to ICCV 2025
Code (To be Realeased Upon Acceptance)

We present General Vision Expert Transformer for Robot Learning (VER), a novel framework that distills knowledge from multiple vision foundation models (DINOv2, ViT, CLIP) into a unified expert.

Abstract

While pretrained vision foundation models (VFMs) substantially advance robotic systems by providing rich, generalized representations, each foundation model remains narrowly specialized, making any single VFM inadequate for the broad range of robotic tasks. Existing methods that distill multiple VFMs into a unified representation pose additional challenges, including lack of flexibility in selecting task-specific representations and the necessity for full retraining when integrating new VFMs or robot-specific domain knowledge. To address these challenges, we propose VER, a Vision Expert transformer for Robot learning. VER distills knowledge from multiple Vision Foundation Models (VFMs) into a specialized expert library, and enable learning dynamic routing to selectively utilize the most task-relevant vision experts for downstream robot tasks. We further investigate the routing mechanism and observe that the router progressively learns to extract lower-entropy, structured task-relevant features, acting as an implicit planning module that alleviates the policy head\'s burden. Moreover, VER facilitates the seamless integration of new trainable experts, enabling the acquisition of robot-related domain knowledge beyond the existing dataset and continuously distilling new vision foundation models. Extensive experiments on 11 diverse robotic benchmarks demonstrate that VER achieves state-of-the-art performance with a 74.7% average success rate.

Method

Our framework consists of two main components: a Base Vision Transformer (BVT) that generates unified feature representations from input images, and a Vision Expert Library (VEL) comprising specialized experts that capture diverse visual representations from various vision foundation models.

Training consists of two phases that enables efficient knowledge distillation from diverse vision foundation models and adaptive feature selection for specific robotic tasks:

(1) Pretraining, where a Base Vision Transformer processes input images, and Teacher-Specific Routers in the Vision Expert Library select appropriate experts to mimic representations from foundation models;

\( \mathcal{L}_{distill}=\sum_{i=1}^{I}\alpha_i[\beta \mathcal{L}_{cos}(h_i(g(f(x), i)), t_i(x)) + (1-\beta) \mathcal{L}_{sL1}(h_i(g(f(x), i)), t_i(x))], \)

\( \mathcal{L}_{mutual}=-\sum_{n=1}^{N}\sum_{i=1}^I p(\mathcal{I}_i,\mathcal{E}_l^n) \cdot \log \frac{p(\mathcal{I}_i,\mathcal{E}_l^n)}{p(\mathcal{I}_i)p(\mathcal{E}_l^n)} \)

\( \mathcal{L}_{pretrain}=\mathcal{L}_{distill}+\gamma\mathcal{L}_{mutual} \)

(2) Downstream Robot Tasks, where we freeze the experts and train a lightweight Robot Router (< 0.4 parameters) that dynamically selects task-relevant visual features to guide the policy head in generating appropriate robotic actions. We also incorporate a noisy gating mechanism that promotes exploration during training.
